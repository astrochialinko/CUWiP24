COVID-19 Disclaimer

This is an update on the COVID-19 measures that CUWiP2022 UA Local Organization Committee is putting in place for CUWiP2022 to ensure the safety of participants during the ongoing pandemic. We are monitoring the evolving public health situation both locally and globally and will adjust our operating procedures accordingly. In addition to following all applicable CDC and UA guidelines, we may take additional measures that we deem necessary. The following guidelines apply for the UA Biosphere conference site as of Aug 31, 2021 and until further notice.


To interact with other conference participants is an important component of CUWiP. Because in our indoor settings adequate continuous distancing is not possible, we will require face masks be worn in all indoor spaces. Outdoors we strongly encourage masks to be worn. We also ask that people spread out as much as possible for events in the main auditorium room.


If at any time after arrival in Tucson, a participant or a member of their travel group starts to exhibit COVID-like symptoms, the participant should do the following: notify a conference organizer so that we can potentially help with contact tracing efforts, not come to the conference site, schedule a COVID test, and follow all CDC and Pima County Public Health rules and guidelines. If anyone in the travel group tests positive for COVID-19 or variant thereof, they and their travel group should not come to the conference site and should quarantine in their accommodations in compliance with CDC guidelines. Should this happen, or any other illness/injury arise late in the participant’s scheduled stay, Biosphere will not be able to provide extended housing. We will do our best to help identify alternative accommodations, and you will be responsible for obtaining and paying for the accommodations and other expenses required for your extended stay.


While the conference organizers are taking these measures to minimize risk, we will be relying on participants and their travel groups to behave responsibly. As such, conference organizers cannot guarantee the safety of participants, and they should understand that they are assuming risk by attending. Participants who repeatedly or egregiously violate the conference’s safety protocols will be asked to leave and will not receive a refund.


While these safety measures will clearly make this year’s CUWiP experience different, we aim to provide the usual inspiring CUWiP experience, with plenty of opportunities to network, exchange ideas, and discuss physics and any interesting topic in person while you are at the conference.


We will continue to monitor the evolving public health situation and hope that some of the measures outlined here might be further eased over the course of the year.


The content of this disclaimer is subject to change owing to the evolving nature of the pandemic. We recommend you frequently check this site.
