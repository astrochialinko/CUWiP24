COVID-19 Disclaimer

Please check out the APS Updated CUWiP Covid Protocol.

This is an update on the COVID-19 measures that CUWiP2022 UA Local Organization Committee is putting in place for CUWiP2022 to ensure the safety of participants during the on-going pandemic. We are monitoring the evolving public health situation both locally and globally and will adjust our operating procedures accordingly. In addition to following all applicable CDC and UA guidelines, we may take additional measures that we deem necessary. The following guidelines apply for the UA Biosphere 2 conference site as of October 22, 2021 and until further notice.


To interact with other conference participants is an important component of CUWiP. To comply with federal and local requirements, all conference participants must be fully vaccinated. Face coverings are required on conference site for all indoor spaces where it is not possible to adequately and continuously maintain social distance.


If at any time after arrival in Tucson, a participant or a member of their travel group starts to exhibit COVID-like symptoms, the participant will have to notify the conference organizer, schedule a COVID test, and be quarantined in a separate casita. If anyone in the travel group tests positive, they and their travel group should not come to the conference site and should quarantine in their accommodations in compliance with CDC guidelines. Should this happen late in the participant’s scheduled stay, quarantine housing will arranged until the quarantine period has ended and they can safely travel. The participant will be responsible for the housing charge of additional time.


While the conference organizers are taking these measures to minimize risk, we will be relying on participants and their travel groups to behave responsibly. As such, conference organizers cannot guarantee the safety of participants, and they should understand that they are assuming risk by attending. Participants who repeatedly or egregiously violate the conference’s safety protocols will be asked to leave and will not receive a refund.


While these safety measures will clearly make this year’s CUWiP experience different, we aim to provide the usual inspiring CUWiP experience, with plenty of opportunities to network, exchange ideas, and discuss physics and any interesting topic in person while you are at the conference.


We will continue to monitor the evolving public health situation and hope that some of the measures outlined here might be further eased over the course of the year.


The content of this disclaimer is subject to change owing to the evolving nature of the pandemic. We recommend you frequently check this site.
